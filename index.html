During the summer of 2020, I decided to learn ROS by creating a few projects. Through the process, I thoroughly learned the fundamentals of ROS as well as Linux (Ubuntu). I also learned how to use Gazebo, the simulation environment mainly used by ROS.

ROS Projects

These two projects were created using ROS.

First, I created an autonomous differential drive robot. The robot has inertia, joints, and a lidar laser sensor. A map of the world was created using a technique called Gmapping, which uses laser range data to make a map. I added a global and local planner to my robot, which plan paths to travel to the provided goal. Another package called AMCL was implemented to account for the drift of the robot due to minor inaccuracies in sensor data like wheel encoders.

Secondly, I created a wall-following robot that follows the curve of a wall or track. I made a Node that publishes a command velocity to the robot based on lidar data. The actual code uses two PID controllers to compute the required velocities to follow the wall.

Amazon Alexa Skill

In the Fall of 2020, two friends and I decided to participate in Auburn Hacks, a hackathon where you must create a project in under 24 hours. We went in with no expectations and no idea of what we were going to make. Less than 24 hours later, we left with a fully functioning Alexa Skill.

The Alexa Skill we made is a categories game. First, you ask Alexa to "play categories game". You can then select a category: fruits, NFL teams, or Minecraft blocks. You and Alexa will then take turns saying words from the category and the first unable to come up with a valid answer loses. Alexa keeps the score, and lets you know how you did at the end.

While this seems like a simple project, behind the simple voice commands is a ton of advanced technology (and room for error). From your intents with words to mishearing you, there is a lot more to creating an Alexa skill than we originally anticipated. In the end, I learned a ton about the importance of teamwork and persistance, and I gained a solid understanding in the workings of an Alexa Skill.

Astro Aero Robotics Club (AARC)

AARC is a club that focuses on mobility systems, multi-agent systems, and systems that can survive in extreme environments. In the fall of 2020, we entered the 2021 NASA RASC-AL Special Edition competition. The task is to design a water extraction system for mars that must identify the layers undeath the surface. During the fall semester, the team designed the system, and in December the team was selected as 1 of 12 finalists that actually get to build the system with NASA's funding. In the spring semester of 2021, AARC will be building the system that we hope will win the competition!
I am a member of the Controls and Telemetry subteam within AARC. We build systems that can control the machine both with manual input and autonomously. Telemetry data is gathered from many sensors which provide feedback for the control system.
